# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who take video meetings (Zoom, Teams, Meet) while working out on a treadmill or exercise bike. They want to stay active during long meeting days without hiding their fitness activity. The overlay shows colleagues that they're multitasking intentionally, not just being unprofessional. Meeting participants see the overlay as part of the camera feed—it signals "I'm here and engaged, just also getting my steps in."

## Product Purpose

Virtual Camera Overlay connects to Home Assistant via WebSocket to display live fitness metrics (steps, speed, distance, heart rate) as a transparent overlay on a webcam feed using OBS virtual camera. Success means being able to stay active during meetings while maintaining professional presence. The overlay communicates effort and intentionality without being distracting or requiring explanation.

## Brand Personality

Playful, personal, unapologetic. The overlay owns the "working out during meetings" lifestyle without being obnoxious about it. It's quietly confident—showing you're active and productive, not making a performance of it.

## Anti-references

- **Generic fitness apps** (Strava, MyFitnessPal): avoid the predictable blue/teal palettes and sterile metric displays
- **Medical/clinical UIs**: not a hospital monitor or health dashboard—this is entertainment
- **Gamer RGB chaos**: no aggressive neon rainbow or over-animated effects that compete with the stream content

## Design Principles

1. **Meeting-appropriate**: visible enough to show you're active, subtle enough not to distract from the meeting itself
2. **Glanceable context**: colleagues understand what the overlay means without you having to explain it
3. **Personality over polish**: quirky details and expressive touches reflect your style, not corporate blandness
4. **Energy follows effort**: visual intensity scales with heart rate zones and activity level—others can see when you're working hard
5. **Non-disruptive by default**: widgets are present but don't compete for attention; meeting content stays primary

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Large text sizing: readable in video meeting thumbnails and gallery view where camera feeds are small
- Readable contrast against varied lighting conditions and backgrounds (home office, gym, outdoor)
- Semi-transparent overlays with sufficient backdrop blur to ensure visibility
- Heart rate color zones use both color and visual weight (not color-dependent alone)
